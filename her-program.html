This Program is Built Around Your PCOS

PCOS causes insulin resistance, which makes fat loss harder through diet alone. The good news: strength training is the single most effective intervention for improving insulin sensitivity — more than cardio. This program prioritises lifting heavy (safely), keeps cardio moderate to avoid cortisol spikes, and uses walking as a powerful daily fat-loss tool. No crash dieting. No punishing workouts. Hormonal balance first, results follow.

Phase 01 // Months 1–3

Foundation

The goal here is to rebuild movement patterns safely, protect the lower back, and build consistency. No hero workouts — just showing up three times a week and walking daily. Research shows even 8,000 steps/day reduces visceral fat significantly in women with PCOS.

Lower Back Protocol All exercises in Phase 1 are back-safe. No conventional deadlifts. No heavy spinal loading. Core stability work (bird dogs, dead bugs, glute bridges) is prioritised because a strong core protects the lower back. If anything causes lower back pain — stop, rest, and modify. 📉 Week 12 — Full Deload

Reduce all weights by 40–50%, same exercises and reps. Also reduce walks to 20 minutes. Your body uses this week to reset hormones, reduce inflammation, and consolidate fat loss. For women with PCOS this is especially important — chronic training stress worsens cortisol and androgens.

Weeks 1–4
Habit Building
Focus entirely on showing up. Don't track weight on a scale — it fluctuates with PCOS hormones. Take waist and hip measurements instead every 4 weeks.
Weeks 5–8
First Load Increases
Add small loads to leg press, lat pulldown, chest press. Core exercises get longer holds. Walking pace increases slightly.
Weeks 9–11
Volume Increase
Move from 2 to 3 sets per exercise. Incline walk duration goes to 20 min post-session. Energy levels improve noticeably here.
Week 12
Deload + Assess
Take measurements. Note how your energy, mood and sleep have changed — these are often the first signs of hormonal improvement with PCOS.

📉 Deload Every 4th Week from Here On

Week 4, 8, and 12 of every phase are deload weeks. For PCOS specifically — this is essential. Prolonged training stress elevates cortisol and androgens, which can worsen symptoms. Deloads allow the body to re-regulate and convert all the hard work into actual fat loss and hormonal improvement.

Support Guide

Nutrition + PCOS

Nutrition for PCOS is different to standard weight loss advice. Insulin resistance is the root issue — so carbohydrate quality, meal timing, and protein intake matter far more than just cutting calories.

💜 The PCOS Nutrition Framework

Insulin Resistance
The core issue. Carbs spike insulin, which in PCOS is already dysregulated. Choose low-GI carbs always (oats, sweet potato, brown rice, lentils). Avoid white bread, white rice, sugary drinks.
Protein Priority
Protein does not spike insulin. It blunts hunger, preserves muscle, and keeps you full. Every meal should have a palm-sized protein source — chicken, eggs, Greek yogurt, fish, tofu.
Anti-Inflammatory
PCOS is an inflammatory condition. Prioritise: berries, leafy greens, fatty fish, olive oil, nuts. Limit: processed foods, vegetable oils, refined sugar, alcohol.
No Crash Dieting
Severe restriction raises cortisol, increases androgens, and worsens PCOS symptoms. A small, consistent deficit of 300–400 cal/day is the evidence-based approach.
Daily Targets
Calories
~1,950
Estimated maintenance for your stats and activity level is ~2,300 cal. A deficit of 300–400 cal/day produces 0.3–0.5 kg/week fat loss — the sustainable, PCOS-friendly rate.
Protein
115–145g
1–1.25g per kg of bodyweight. This is higher than most women are told to eat. It preserves muscle, improves satiety, and has no negative effect on PCOS hormones.
Carbs
Low-GI Only
Don't eliminate carbs — just choose quality ones. Sweet potato, oats, legumes, brown rice, quinoa, whole fruit. Time them around training for best insulin response.
Water
3–4 L/day
PCOS causes water retention — staying well hydrated actually reduces bloating, not increases it. Drink 500ml before every training session.
Meal Timing
Every 3–4 hrs
Eating regularly prevents blood sugar crashes that spike cortisol and cravings. Don't skip meals. Eat within 2 hours of waking. Always eat before a workout — fasted training raises cortisol in PCOS.
Sleep
7–9 hrs
Sleep deprivation significantly worsens insulin resistance and increases androgens in PCOS. Sleep is not optional — it's a core part of this program.
PCOS Supplements (Evidence-Based)
Inositol (Myo + D-Chiro)
4g / 100mg
The most researched PCOS supplement. Improves insulin sensitivity, restores ovulatory cycles, reduces androgens. A 40:1 ratio of Myo-inositol to D-chiro-inositol is the clinically validated dose. Speak to your doctor first.
Magnesium Glycinate
300–400mg
Improves insulin sensitivity and reduces cortisol. Many women with PCOS are deficient. Take before bed — also improves sleep quality.
Vitamin D3 + K2
2,000 IU D3
Vitamin D deficiency is very common in PCOS and worsens insulin resistance. Get levels tested. Take with a fat-containing meal. K2 helps direct the calcium properly.
Omega-3 (Fish Oil)
2–3g EPA/DHA
Reduces inflammation and androgens in PCOS. High-quality fish oil or algae-based omega-3 if plant-based. One of the most consistent PCOS supplements in research.
Creatine Monohydrate
5g/day
Safe for women. Improves strength, helps build muscle that improves insulin sensitivity. Does not cause bloating or weight gain beyond initial water retention in muscle.
Spearmint Tea
2 cups/day
Clinically shown to reduce free testosterone in PCOS. Reduces hirsutism and may improve the androgen-related symptoms. Cheap, accessible, and backed by multiple RCTs.
Key Principles
Principle 01
Don't Use the Scale as Your Only Metric
PCOS causes hormonal water retention. You can lose significant fat and gain muscle while the scale moves slowly. Track waist and hip measurements every 4 weeks. Take monthly photos. Notice how clothes fit.
Principle 02
Strength Training is Your Most Powerful Tool
More important than cardio for PCOS. Every lifting session improves insulin sensitivity for up to 24 hours. After 6 months of consistent lifting, the metabolic improvement is dramatic.
Principle 03
Walk Every Day — Non-Negotiable
Daily walking is one of the most underrated fat loss tools for PCOS. It doesn't spike cortisol, it improves insulin sensitivity, and 10,000 steps burns an extra 350–500 calories without the body adapting.
Principle 04
Protect Your Lower Back — Always
Never push through lower back pain. Build core strength through anti-extension exercises (dead bugs, bird dogs, planks) before heavy compound lifts. Core strength IS lower back protection.
Principle 05
Stress Management is Part of the Program
Elevated cortisol worsens every PCOS symptom. Find a daily stress reduction habit — walks in nature, breathwork, journalling, 5 min of deep breathing. This is not optional for PCOS recovery.
Principle 06
Work With a Doctor if Possible
Consider seeing an endocrinologist or gynaecologist who specialises in PCOS. Metformin (a prescription medication) is sometimes used alongside lifestyle changes and can dramatically improve insulin resistance. A registered dietitian with PCOS experience is also invaluable.
