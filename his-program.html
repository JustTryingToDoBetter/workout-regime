📉 End of Phase Deload (Week 12)

Week 12 is your first deload week. Reduce all weights by 40%, keep same exercises, same reps. Your body uses this week to consolidate the gains made. Skipping deloads is the #1 mistake returning lifters make — do not skip it.

Week 1–4
Rediscovery
Focus 100% on technique over weight. Pick loads where you could do 5 more reps.
Week 5–8
Progressive Overload Begins
Add 2.5–5kg to compound lifts every 1–2 weeks. Add 1 rep per set before adding weight.
Week 9–11
Volume Increase
Move from 2 sets to 3 sets per exercise. Cardio creeps up to 18 minutes.
Week 12
Deload
Reduce intensity. Assess progress — take photos, note lifts. Prep mentally for Phase 2.

📉 Deload Weeks: Every 4th Week

From Phase 2 onward, program a deload every 4th week automatically. Reduce weights by 40-50%, keep all other variables the same. This is when muscle actually grows — recovery is part of the program.

Key Principles
Principle 01
Protein Is the Priority — Always
Hit 142g protein before worrying about anything else. If you do this consistently, 80% of your nutrition is sorted. Everything else — calories, carbs, fats — is secondary.
Principle 02
Meal Prep on Sundays
Cook 4–5 portions of chicken + rice in one session. Store in containers. This is the single habit that makes consistent eating effortless during the week.
Principle 03
Creatine Every Day, No Exceptions
5g of creatine in your morning water or shake. It works cumulatively — missing days resets the benefit. It's the cheapest and most effective supplement you can buy.
Principle 04
Eat Before You Train
Always eat 60–90 min before a session. Training fasted reduces strength output and increases muscle breakdown. A simple oats + banana is enough.
Principle 05
Don't Drink Your Calories
Avoid sugary drinks, fruit juices, energy drinks. Drink water, black coffee, and rooibos tea. Those liquid calories add up fast without filling you up.
