📉 End of Phase Deload (Week 12)

Week 12 is your first deload week. Reduce all weights by 40%, keep same exercises, same reps. Your body uses this week to consolidate the gains made. Skipping deloads is the #1 mistake returning lifters make — do not skip it.

Week 1–4
Rediscovery
Focus 100% on technique over weight. Pick loads where you could do 5 more reps.
Week 5–8
Progressive Overload Begins
Add 2.5–5kg to compound lifts every 1–2 weeks. Add 1 rep per set before adding weight.
Week 9–11
Volume Increase
Move from 2 sets to 3 sets per exercise. Cardio creeps up to 18 minutes.
Week 12
Deload
Reduce intensity. Assess progress — take photos, note lifts. Prep mentally for Phase 2.

From Phase 2 onward, program a deload every 4th week automatically. Reduce weights by 40-50%, keep all other variables the same. This is when muscle actually grows — recovery is part of the program.

Support Guide

NUTRITION + RECOVERY

Training is the stimulus. Nutrition and sleep are where the actual growth happens. These are evidence-based targets to support muscle gain and endurance simultaneously.

Daily Nutrition Targets
Protein
142–177g
2–2.5g per kg of bodyweight. This is the most important dietary variable for muscle growth. Prioritise chicken, eggs, fish, Greek yogurt, whey.
Calories (Lean Bulk)
~2,800
Estimated maintenance is ~2,450 for your stats and activity. Aim for +200–350 calories above maintenance for a slow, clean bulk. Track with Cronometer or MyFitnessPal.
Sleep
7–9 hrs
More important than any supplement. Growth hormone is released during deep sleep. Under 6 hours = significantly impaired recovery and muscle protein synthesis.
Water
3–4 L/day
Especially important for performance. Dehydration by just 2% impairs strength and endurance. Drink 500ml before training.
Pre-Workout Meal
1–2 hrs Before
Carbs + protein. Example: rice + chicken, or oats + whey. Carbs fuel the session; protein reduces muscle breakdown.
Post-Workout
Within 2 hrs
Protein + carbs. 30–40g protein to maximise muscle protein synthesis. The "anabolic window" is wider than once thought — within 2 hours is fine.
Supplement Basics (Evidence-Based)
Creatine Monohydrate
5g/day
The most researched supplement in history. Proven to increase strength, power, and muscle volume. Take it daily, any time. No loading phase needed.
Whey Protein
As needed
Only use if you struggle to hit protein targets from food. Not magic — just convenient protein. Isolate if you're lactose sensitive.
Caffeine
3–6mg/kg
Proven ergogenic aid. ~200–400mg 30–45 min before training. Black coffee works. Avoid within 6 hours of sleep.
Vitamin D3
1,000–2,000 IU
Critical for testosterone production and immune function. Many South Africans are still deficient due to indoor lifestyles. Take with a meal containing fat.
Arm Growth — The Key Principles
Why your Arms Will Grow Arms are a "small" muscle group trained frequently in this program via compound pulling (biceps) and pushing (triceps) movements, PLUS direct isolation. The tricep makes up 2/3 of arm size — so heavy close-grip benching, overhead extensions and skull crushers are just as important as curls. For the bicep peak specifically: incline curls (long head stretch) are the most important exercise. Train each arm muscle with 12–20 sets/week across the program phases. Stretch-mediated hypertrophy (training at long muscle lengths) is prioritised — this is why overhead extensions and incline curls appear in every phase.
Key Principles to Follow
Principle 01
Progressive Overload is the Law
Every week, aim to either add weight, add reps, or add a set. If none of those happened, the workout was maintenance — not growth.
Principle 02
Train Consistently Over Train Perfectly
An imperfect session you show up to beats a perfect program you skip. Over 2 years, the lifters who progress are the consistent ones — not the most talented ones.
Principle 03
Don't Skip Deloads
Your muscles grow during recovery. Deload weeks (reduced intensity, same movements) allow supercompensation. Program them every 4th week from Phase 2 onward.
Principle 04
Film Your Lifts
Record compound lifts from the side. Form breakdown is the number one injury cause — watching yourself is free coaching.
Principle 05
Lift Then Cardio
Always lift before cardio. Cardio after resistance training has minimal effect on muscle gains. Cardio before lifting impairs strength output.
Principle 06
Track Everything
Keep a training log — even a notes app works. Record weights, sets, and reps every session. This is how you beat last week's numbers.
